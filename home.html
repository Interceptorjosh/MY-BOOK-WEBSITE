Igede Evolution (A Documentary of the people of Igede)

The Igede people are among the three distinct languages in the Benue state, numerically smaller than the Tub and Idomia. Although they have been able to make their presence felt in the economic, educational and political life of the state. Due to their co-existence, the Igede have believes that they have migrated from Sabogina Ora in Ohain 1 West Local Government area of Edo State. Over 400 years ago through their forefathers who is known as AGBA who is said to be the eleven sons of the Ora Epen Twelve Children. The Ora Crown was found by prince opame one of the grandchildren of the sons of Oba Ewen. The great of Benin between 1440-1474 AD, Prince Opame who later became the oba of Benin was exile because of his behaviors he left Benin with some of his friends, one of them named Obusulua and Zanke in there they settled at the eduerere, around the middle of the 15 century, at eduerere he settle and married a lady named OZORULUA who gave birth to son name Ora epen. Ora epen in turn gave birth to twelve recorded sons, names are listed according to seniority which are Umale,Ahiedu,Akhuaize,Oba,Ivbiabe,Eghonmi,Owato,Uguan,Roba,Odu,Ohela, Agba and Ofebe. Unfortunate, Umale the first son was beaten to death by his brothers for been neglecting, the remaining eleven son’s forms up to the six sub-clans of ora, the clans and how they were founded and listed as follow.

OHIA/UHIRE clan founded by AHIEDU and AGBA
OVIBIOKHUANIN clan founded by AKHUAIZE
EVBIOBE(Sabogina) clan founded by OBE and ODU Later joined by IYERE
UHONMORA clan founded by OHELA and EGHONMI
OKE clan founded by OWATO and
EME clan founded by UGUAN ROBA and OFEBE
Agba was a traditional Medicinal man and Herbalist who has followers that play musical drums called "OGEDE". This drum was double side then Agba was nickname OBA OGEDE which is interpreted as native doctor which plays OGEDE drum, from where the "Igede" people must have derived their names, it is also known that AGBA was a renowned trader who travelled as far as Asaba, Ida, Ihe-ife and the Old Kwararafa Kingdom which covers some Taraba, Kogi, Benue and part of Nasarrawa state today and beyond the River Niger. One of his trip to Ife, he makes friends with the people and was initiated with the chieftaincy institution which arousal mouth of his interest. On his return from Ife he then introduce the chieftaincy to his brothers and ora in general which later spread to whole of owan, he was also believed that on one of his trip to Kwararafa Kingdom, He decided to marry and settle down. Contrary to the popular means that Igede people were chased out from ora in owan due to a fight over a broken pot. Their fathers AGBA who was a traveler only decided to settle at the place in which the Igede are founded today. It is of a known fact that all ora people are direct descendant of oba ozulua, the emperor of Benin Kingdom who reign between 1481 to 1503 AD this means that AGBA and all the Igede children are of the royal blood of oba of Benin Kingdom.

BRIEF HISTORY OF IGEDE EVOLUTION

Our father Agba was a renowned tourist, he was a business man to the core of the early days, he was at ife, the interest he gather there was the Yoruba initiative people with chieftaincy titles both interested and beset to quick safe return to his father's land. This land is ORA EPEN the father of ORA, we only shorten it as ora but our father which is your father also is ORA EPEN. In short, these ora Epen was brought to this world by OPAME, Prince Opame, the son of Oba Abel the Ogidiga. The King of Benin , he had a son called Opame, because of his behaviors he did a lot of things the Benin kingdom could not bear, been a prince they could not do anything to him order than he should leave the land. He was exile in the middle 15 century; he left Benin with some friends, uncle with carriers of drums, well wisher and laborers clearing the foot paths. All these people who followed him down there settle there and they are known as the Owen people today. We have two local government which are Owen west and Owen east but they came back to his son that is known as ora open, before he left Benin his father allowed him to leave Benin with mixed feelings, he told the Benin people that these boy you are exiling today his the only one that could save Benin in the future from problem since you say he should go let him go in peace, he then left Benin. We are the children of Agba the eleven son of Ora Epen. As I earlier said, when he finish with Ife, he went to Asaba, to Ida and others,probably that was how he settle there, he was a herbalist, herbalist with a difference that he has people who is playing drum for him whenever he is going out. These drums have two edges, which his known as "OGEGE" probably you derived your name from “OGEGE” to be "IGEDE". The drum, instead of calling it OGEGE you call it IGEDE over there. This is your land; you are at home you should feel at home whenever in Benin. By and By I don't say you should leave Benin but will be transferring home gradually as our forefathers left Benin later became ozuolua as the father earlier said because when his father died instead of serving after him they took one of his brother after the throne that is OBA ORUA, Orua sat on the throne only for fourteen (14) days. He was sick during his coronation when he died, They took his second brother and put him on the throne that is OBA IZORTI, that one mounted the throne, when he mounted there, there was no more peace in Benin kingdom, there was hunger, famine, that if you plant corn nor yam it won't grow, Benin kingdom was diminishing so they went to consult an oracle who told them that the owner of the kingdom is somewhere they should look for him, they look for him quite alright and brought him, asking him to come back to Benin to take the throne, he laughs and said No and tell them that you people know am still alive and you put the wrong person on the throne, I will not come back, he took them three good years to convince him to come back and take the throne of Benin. After the third years he made up his way, he decided to go after they sent him a parable that made him understand that wherever you are your home is your home. They collected two rat, a bush rat and a house rat and a calabash them and send it to him, why am I telling you to come home this is what am telling you now, when they got to him, he said you people are here again they said yes we will continue to come until you come to Benin which is your home, they told him this our present for you, he said I don't know the content inside that they should open it, they open the first one which contain a bush rat, the bush rat jump down looking for an escape they were looking at the rat, he run to the bush fast, they open the second one, that one contained an house rat. The house rat come out went to the gate smelt it and come back to the room, he was looking for the stick because in those days when house are been built they normally put two or three sticks and roof it, when he just get one of the stick he just climb it and run to the decking at the top, that is it home. So our father now said to him, Prince Opame wherever you go your home is your home. He left and come back and was crown the Oba of Benin in 1483, he rule for Seventeen (17) years and died in 1500. When he died our father the father of your father AGBA ORA EPEN now went for the royal burial ceremony. When he got there he participated in everything, after the burial they were looking at him and he was looking at them and he ask them why are you people wasting time that he is my father’s too I wants to stay there, they said No, he said why, they answered they will inform him in nine (9) days time. On the Ninth day, they gather together, the chiefs and elders of Benin to pass the words to him that they don't deny that he his son of oba of ozuolua who died but he was not born in Benin that he was born outside Benin. He said you people mean it they said yes, that why you people said I should not sit on these throne, this is my father’s sit and I will occupy it, anyone who knows his father's sit should come and occupy it, he said, he lived in Benin for good four (4) years even Benin was without king during the period in history recorded, after the fourth year, he said why am I wasting time that it is time for him to go since he wasn't allowed to sit on his father throne that he will leave, Benin became afraid because his father was a warrior, a conqueror and a successful warrior, they thought he will come to Benin anytime to wage war, he said No that his is going back to his land to meet his children that was how he left them, before 1504 another oba was put there. So, if you are in Benue State the royal blue blood is in each an every one of you, you are all prince and princesses in Benue State. Benue is not just the ordinary Benin’s, the royal blue blood flows in your veins. If any Benin man meets you in Benue State go back and tell him that you are descendants of oba ozuolua, Ibe Ozuolua that is what we call ourselves that is the children of Ozuolua, and if you tell any Benin man he will change his mind and start giving you your due respect. We are not ordinary Benin people but prince and princess of Benin Kingdom. All I want to tell each and every one of you, you must know who you are where you come and must be proud of whom you are. One day you will realize your journey.
